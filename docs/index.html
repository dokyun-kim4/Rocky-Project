Stationary Balancing:

With the current control loop setup, only the angle gets corrected. Due to this, Rocky doesn’t stay in place and will drive as far as needed to keep the angle stable, resulting in it colliding with obstacles and moving unpredictably. To minimize Rocky’s positional variation, another feedback loop block, J(s), is added between K(s) and H(s) to control velocity and position of the cart.

P1/P2: -3.2579 ± 3.3238i

P3: -7 + 0i

P4/P5: -3.7234 ± 2.7925i

For pole pair P1/P2, a of 0.7 was used, and for pole pair P3/P4, a  of 0.8 was used. This creates poles that are slightly underdampened, allowing for some oscillation as the Rocky reaches steady state.
